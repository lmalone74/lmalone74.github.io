<h2>Professional Self-Assessment</h2>

<p>
This portfolio represents the culmination of my work and learning over the past three and a half years while completing my bachelor’s degree in Computer Science at Southern New Hampshire University. I currently work as a lead in a Client Escalations role, where I have developed strong experience in team development and client service.
My goal is to expand my role within my company by combining my in-depth knowledge of our products and processes with my technical skills in software development to help design, improve, and support the applications we use every day, as well as streamline internal workflows. I also look forward to dedicating more time to developing my own indie games now that I have completed my coursework. This portfolio demonstrates the growth of my skills and my ability to apply them in practical, real-world scenarios. 
</p>

<p>
As I progressed through my degree program, I found that the skills I developed carried over into my current non-technical role. Breaking down complex processes into structured steps and diagramming workflows reflects the planning techniques I learned while designing programs. I have learned that creating a clear plan improves efficiency while still allowing flexibility as requirements evolve.
Throughout the program, I also discovered that with enough research and persistence, I can solve most technical challenges. With access to resources for languages and tools such as Python, C++, and Unreal Blueprints, I became more confident in my ability to learn and adapt. I applied these skills to improve earlier work, such as transitioning the Grazioso Salvare project from a Jupyter Notebook into a more structured, full-stack implementation.
</p>

<p>
While opportunities for team-based work were limited, I gained valuable experience in GAM-305, where I worked as part of a team to develop a 2.5D side-scrolling game level within five weeks. As Team Lead, I contributed across multiple areas, including animation, sound, and 3D modeling, while also managing the team’s communication with our stakeholder, the professor, through weekly updates. This experience strengthened my ability to balance leadership with technical work and reinforced the importance of adaptability and collaboration in meeting shared goals.
</p>

<p>
Communicating with stakeholders has been an important skill I developed throughout the program. I created documentation, presentations, and recorded walkthroughs to explain technical concepts such as system design, cloud implementations, and security in a way that could be understood by non-technical audiences. In my current role, I apply these skills by developing training materials, including PowerPoint presentations and instructional videos, to support both clients and internal teams. This has improved my ability to present complex information clearly and effectively.
Data structures and algorithms have shaped how I approach problem-solving and efficiency in my code. I worked with structures such as vectors, arrays, and classes, and implemented algorithms including sorting and iterative calculations. In the Airgead Banking application, I used vectors to manage multiple investment scenarios and applied compound interest calculations. These experiences reinforced the importance of efficiency, scalability, and structured data management.
Software engineering and database design have been key areas of growth, particularly in building structured and maintainable applications. I developed experience with object-oriented programming, modular design, and refactoring code to improve organization. In the Grazioso Salvare project, I worked with MongoDB and transitioned the application into a modular system with separate components for database interaction, data handling, and user interface logic. This improved both usability and maintainability while reinforcing full-stack and client-server concepts.
Security has also been an important focus in my development. I learned to protect sensitive information, control access, and reduce vulnerabilities through proper design. In the Grazioso Salvare project, I replaced hardcoded credentials with environment variables, aligning with secure configuration practices. I also gained exposure to authentication, role-based access control, and secure communication, reinforcing the importance of security as part of the development process.
</p>

<p>
As I move forward in my career, my goal is to transition into a more technical role where I can combine my client service experience with software development to build tools that improve efficiency and usability. I value clear problem-solving, structured design, and creating solutions that are practical and maintainable, while continuing to develop skills in cloud technologies, AI, and software design.
The artifacts in this portfolio demonstrate my growth across key areas of computer science. <a href="/artifact1.html">Artifact One</a> focuses on software design and algorithmic implementation through a C++ game application, highlighting object-oriented design and user-focused improvements. <a href="/artifact2.html">Artifact Two</a> demonstrates data structures and algorithms through the Airgead Banking application, including vector-based data handling and enhanced reporting. <a href="/artifact3.html">Artifact Three</a> showcases database and full-stack development through the Grazioso Salvare project, emphasizing modular design and improved security practices. Together, these artifacts represent my progression from writing functional programs to designing more complete, structured, and secure systems.
This portfolio shows not only what I have learned throughout my degree, but also how I have been able to apply those skills in practical ways. I have developed a solid foundation in problem-solving, software design, and technical communication. It demonstrates that I am ready to step into a technical role and continue my growth as a developer.
</p>
